# Customer-Insights-Analysis

**Executive Summary**

Using Python, SQL, and Power BI, I analyzed customer shopping data to identify key revenue drivers. The analysis showed that purchase frequency and product categories impact revenue more than demographics. I built dashboards to track customer KPIs and recommended targeted marketing, loyalty programs, and strategic promotions, with potential to increase customer retention and overall revenue.

**Business Problem**

Retail businesses often struggle to understand what truly drives customer purchases and revenue, leading to inefficient marketing strategies and missed growth opportunities. Without clear insights into customer behavior, purchase frequency, and product performance, companies risk underutilizing high-value customers and overspending on low-impact segments. The purpose of this project is to identify key factors influencing customer spending and engagement, enabling the business to make data-driven decisions to increase revenue, improve targeting, and enhance customer retention.


**Methodology**

1. Cleaned and prepared customer shopping data using Python and SQL.

2. Performed exploratory data analysis to examine patterns across purchase frequency, product categories, customer segments, and spending behavior.

3. Analyzed key KPIs such as average purchase value, customer distribution, and engagement metrics.

**Skills**

1. SQL: joins, filtering, aggregations, grouping

2. Python: Numpy, Pandas, Data Cleaning, Feature Engineering, Exploratory Data Analysis

3. Power BI: DAX, Charts, KPI Creation

**Results and Business Recommendation**

The analysis was conducted on 3,900 customer transactions, with an average purchase amount of $59.76 and an average review rating of 3.75 .

Revenue analysis shows that male customers generate $157K in revenue compared to $75K from female customers, indicating higher spending contribution. Across product categories, clothing is the top-performing category ($104K revenue, 1,737 sales), followed by accessories ($74K), while outerwear contributes the least.

Customer segmentation reveals that 80% of customers are loyal (3,116 customers), with only 83 new customers, highlighting strong retention but limited new customer acquisition. Subscription analysis shows that non-subscribers contribute the majority of revenue ($170K vs $62K from subscribers), despite similar average spend ($59).

Shipping behavior shows Express shipping customers spend slightly more ($60.48 vs $58.46), and young adults generate the highest revenue ($62K) among all age groups. Additionally, products like hats and sneakers have ~50% discount usage, indicating high dependency on promotions.

Overall, product category, customer loyalty, and purchase behavior (frequency, discounts, and shipping type) are the key drivers of revenue and sales performance.
